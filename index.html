Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between drywall and POP ceilings, and which one is better? +

Drywall (also known as plasterboard) is a panel made of gypsum plaster pressed between two thick sheets of paper. It's used to make interior walls and ceilings. PoP (Plaster of Paris) is a building material used for coating walls and ceilings. PoP is generally considered better for decorative ceilings as it allows for more intricate designs and patterns, while drywall is typically used for creating flat surfaces and partitions. The choice depends on your specific needs, aesthetic preferences, and budget.

The timeline for PoP ceiling installation depends on the complexity of the design and the size of the area. A standard residential installation typically takes 3-7 days. More intricate designs or larger commercial projects may take longer. We provide a detailed timeline during our initial consultation.
